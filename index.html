How Buying a New Apartment in Israel Works
A complete step-by-step guide for overseas buyers
01
Find Your Property
Browse our portfolio of 55+ new developments across 15 Israeli cities. We'll help you narrow down based on your budget, lifestyle, community preferences, and investment goals.
02
Hire an Israeli Lawyer
This is non-negotiable. Every buyer must have independent legal representation in Israel. We connect you with trusted English-speaking lawyers who specialize in overseas buyer transactions.
03
Sign a Reservation Agreement
Once you've chosen your unit, you sign a reservation agreement and typically pay a small reservation fee (₪20,000–₪50,000) to take the unit off the market while your lawyer reviews the full contract.
04
Contract Review & Due Diligence
Your lawyer reviews the purchase contract, checks the developer's building permits, bank guarantee arrangements, and any planning issues. This typically takes 2–4 weeks.
05
Sign the Purchase Contract
Once your lawyer is satisfied, you sign the official purchase contract (Chozeh Rechisha). The payment schedule begins — typically 20% on signing, with the balance paid in stages or at delivery.
06
Pay Purchase Tax (Mas Rechisha)
As a foreign buyer purchasing your first Israeli property, you currently pay 8% purchase tax on the full property value. In designated areas like Giv'at Ze'ev, this is fully waived.
07
Construction Period & Updates
Holy Homes and your lawyer keep you informed throughout construction. All payments are protected by bank guarantees — if the developer defaults, your money is returned in full.
08
Pre-Delivery Inspection
Before receiving keys, you (or a representative) conduct a formal inspection of the apartment. Any defects or deviations from the specification are documented and the developer is legally required to rectify them.
09
Final Payment & Key Handover
The final payment (usually the remaining balance) is made upon key handover. Your lawyer registers the property in your name at the Israeli Land Registry (Tabu or Minhal).
10
Welcome Home!
You now own property in Eretz Yisrael. 🇮🇱 Holy Homes can also connect you with interior designers, furniture suppliers, rental management companies, and anything else you need.
Frequently Asked Questions
The most common questions from American buyers
Can Americans buy property in Israel?
+

Yes, absolutely. There are no restrictions on foreign nationals purchasing real estate in Israel. Americans, Israelis living abroad, and people of all nationalities can buy Israeli property. The process is well-established and fully legal.

Can I get a mortgage in Israel as a foreign buyer?
+

Yes. Israeli banks do offer mortgages to foreign nationals, including Americans. The amount you can borrow is typically up to 50% of the property value (compared to 75% for Israeli residents). You'll need to provide proof of income, bank statements, and other documentation. Our mortgage broker partners specialize in foreign buyer applications.

How much is the purchase tax (Mas Rechisha)?
+

For a foreign buyer purchasing their first property in Israel, purchase tax is currently 8% on the full property value. However, in designated investment areas like Giv'at Ze'ev, foreign buyers are fully exempt. This exemption can save ₪500,000 or more on a mid-range purchase. Ask us for details on which properties qualify.

How are payments protected during construction?
+

Under Israeli law (Law 5734-1974), developers must provide a bank guarantee for every payment received during construction. This means if the developer defaults or construction is delayed indefinitely, your money is returned in full by the guaranteeing bank. Some projects also carry additional guarantees from insurance companies like Clal Insurance.

Do I need to be physically in Israel to buy?
+

No. The entire process can be completed remotely through Power of Attorney (Iyul). Your Israeli lawyer can be appointed to sign documents on your behalf. Many of our overseas clients complete purchases without visiting Israel — though we always recommend our Sukkot tours so you can see properties firsthand before committing.

What are the typical additional costs beyond the purchase price?
+

Typical additional costs include: Purchase Tax (0% in exempt zones, 8% otherwise), Lawyer fees (0.5–1.5% + VAT), Agent commission (2% + VAT — often paid by the developer), Land registration fees, and Property appraisal fee if taking a mortgage. We provide a full cost breakdown for every property before you commit to anything.

Can I rent out my Israeli property when I'm not using it?
+

Yes, in most cases. Israel has a strong rental market, particularly in Jerusalem, Tel Aviv, and coastal cities. Short-term rental (Airbnb-style) is allowed in most areas. However, note that 11 units in our New Boutique in Central Jerusalem project are under the Investment Encouragement Law, which requires a 5-year rental commitment. Free-market units have no such restrictions. Always check the specific conditions of the unit you're purchasing.
